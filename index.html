Thesis · In Five Words

Tell a story. Stay loose.

Good professional speaking is not a monologue you perform. It is a conversation you steer. Every real-world speaking situation I have been in this semester — interviews, pitches, classroom debates, co-op meetings — has rewarded the same three things: a narrative the audience can follow, a small set of anchor points I actually know cold, and enough room to move when the room changes on me.

What follows is the rest of the sentence, split into three chapters.

One Key Takeaway

Stop rehearsing. Start listening.

If I had to collapse this whole semester into a single discipline I will carry into every future meeting, pitch, and interview, it is this: the best thing I can do in the thirty seconds before I open my mouth is stop practicing what I am about to say and start listening to what the room is actually doing.

The script cannot save me in those rooms. The story can. And so can paying attention.
